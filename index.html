🏙️ Downtown LA

Grand Central Market & the DTLA Melting Pot

Start inside the 105-year-old Grand Central Market, where 30+ stalls serve everything from Salvadoran pupusas to artisan cheeseburgers. Then wind through the historic arts district, tasting tacos at spots locals have loved for decades, sipping craft coffee in converted warehouses, and finishing with churros at the oldest Mexican bakery in the city.

🍜 Little Tokyo

Ramen, Wagashi & the Soul of Japanese LA

One of the oldest Japanese communities in the US, Little Tokyo is a treasure of authentic ramen shops, decades-old mochi makers, izakayas, and tiny wagashi (Japanese sweets) workshops. This tour reveals the living history of Japanese-American culture through every bite.

🥩 Koreatown

KBBQ, Banchan & Nighttime Flavors of K-Town

The densest neighborhood in LA is also one of its most electric. We navigate the warren of strip malls and multi-story food halls that house LA's best Korean BBQ, banchan (side dishes), soon tofu jjigae, and the dessert cafes open past midnight. This evening tour is best done hungry.

🌮 Boyle Heights

Old-School Tacos, Tamales & East LA's Heartbeat

Before tacos went trendy, Boyle Heights was already a taco paradise. This tour is a homage to the Mexican-American community that has called this neighborhood home for generations — from 4am birria spots to hand-pressed tortilla ladies to the carnitas stand that hasn't changed since 1967.
